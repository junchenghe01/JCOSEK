# License FAQ (MPL‑2.0)

Q: If I statically link the RTOS into my closed-source firmware, do I have to open my application code?  
A: MPL is file-level copyleft. Only modified MPL‑licensed files must be made available under MPL‑2.0 when distributing binaries. Independent application files that do not modify MPL files may remain closed-source.

Q: How do I comply when distributing firmware that includes modified MPL files?  
A: Provide the modified MPL source files alongside the firmware (e.g., on the product download page) or include them with the distribution. The method must make the source reasonably accessible.

Q: Can I license other parts of the project differently?  
A: Yes—different files or directories can have different licenses. Document the license mapping in `LICENSES.md` or README to avoid confusion.

Q: Is MPL suitable for commercial use?  
A: Yes. MPL allows commercial use; it only requires that modifications to MPL‑licensed files are published under MPL‑2.0.

If you need specific guidance tailored to your build/distribution flow, share your repo structure and release process and we’ll provide concrete advice.
