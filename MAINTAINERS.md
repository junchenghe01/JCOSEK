## 🛠️ Roles and Responsibilities

We categorize project leadership into two specific roles to keep community operations structured:

### 1. Project Lead (Benevolent Dictator)
- Oversees the overarching technical vision, strategic direction, and roadmap of JCOSEK.
- Holds final tie-breaking decision-making authority over major architectural overhauls or breaking API changes.
- Manages administrative aspects of the repository (secrets, organization access, legal/compliance).

### 2. Module Maintainers
- Review, give feedback, and approve Pull Requests related to their assigned subsystems (e.g., specific hardware drivers or test suites).
- Triaging incoming bugs, labeling issues, and participating in technical discussions under `.github/CODEOWNERS` designations.
- Keep relevant documentation updated as new features under their domain are implemented.

## 📈 Becoming a Maintainer

JCOSEK is a meritocracy. We highly welcome active contributors to join our core maintenance team. The path to becoming a maintainer involves the following steps:

1. **Demonstrate Commitment**: Consistently contribute to the project over a reasonable period (typically 3–6 months) by fixing bugs, implementing features, refactoring code, or significantly improving documentation.
2. **Community Engagement**: Review other contributors' Pull Requests constructively, triage reported issues, and actively engage in technical design talks within the GitHub Discussions forum.
3. **Nomination**: An existing maintainer can nominate you for maintainer status based on your contributions. 
4. **Approval**: The nomination requires approval from the Project Lead. Once confirmed, you will be granted write access to the repository and added to this file as well as the `.github/CODEOWNERS` rules.

## 🚪 Stepping Down & Inactivity Policy

Life priorities change, and we completely understand that maintainers may need to shift focus away from the project. To keep the project moving forward smoothly:

- **Voluntary Retiral**: Maintainers can step down at any time by sending a notice to the team. They will be moved to the **Emeritus Maintainers** registry to honor their historical contributions.
- **Inactivity Policy**: If a maintainer has no visible community activity (commits, PR reviews, issue triaging, discussion responses) for **6 consecutive months**, the Project Lead will reach out to check in. If the maintainer is unresponsive or unable to return to active duty, they will be respectfully transitioned to *Emeritus* status to ensure the `.github/CODEOWNERS` system remains responsive.
